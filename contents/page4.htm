Chapter three

ATLANTA GEORGIA

Center of Disease Control

It only lasted for a second.

But in that brief passage of time, some 500,000,000 people from all over the world died instantaneously. They were all fairskinned people who were caught outside during a sudden burst of undiluted UV rays.

An estimated one billion people had cancer, instantly introduced into their bodies and an estimated half billion were blinded.

Nearly every person on earth was harmed in some degree. The phenomenon will happen again--and again. Each time will last longer and do more damage than the time before. If no solution is found to this threat, the species of man and woman will perish off the face of the earth.
